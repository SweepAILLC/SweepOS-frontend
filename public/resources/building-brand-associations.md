## HOW TO BUILD INTENTIONAL ASSOCIATIONS

- Build Associations, Not Just Content: Your brand is defined by the immediate thoughts people have about you. Intentionally build positive associations (e.g., trustworthy, successful) to create a unique identity and a reason for people to invest.
- Market Outcomes, Not Just Physique: Clients buy the *outcomes* of fitness (confidence, energy, attraction), not just the physique. Content must market these desired outcomes to be effective.
- Treat Content as an Ecosystem: Your content must be a reactive ecosystem with multiple touchpoints (e.g., mindset, value) to guide your audience through the 5 stages of awareness, not a series of isolated posts.
- Use Story Sequences for CTAs: Place Calls to Action (CTAs) in story sequences and carousels, not reel descriptions, to maximize visibility and engagement.

## Topics

### The Power of Associations

- Definition: The immediate thoughts, feelings, and images people associate with your brand.
- Function: A strong brand identity built on positive associations creates trust and a unique selling proposition, giving people a reason to choose you.
- Examples:
    - Josh Chang: Porsche, Miami, Family Man → Trustworthy, successful.
    - Hunter Stein: Calisthenics, Girlfriend, Corvette → Markets the desired outcome of attraction.

Kai Louie-Badua: Athlete, family oriented, software, systems → Grit, trustworthy, smart, credible

### Marketing Outcomes vs. Physique

- Clients buy the *outcomes* of fitness, not just the physique.
- Key Outcomes: Confidence, energy, discipline, attraction, health.
- Example: Hunter Stein's content markets the outcome (attraction) by showing his girlfriend watching his handstands, making the content far more compelling than a solo workout.

My clients want to feel accomplished, professional, and fulfilled in their business. They want to provide for the people in their life and give back. Additionally, they want to be treated as such, whether that's the respect from their families, women, or other entrepreneurs, my clients care more about how their business can positively impact their relationships with people rather than the money.

### Building Intentional Associations

- Strategy: Reverse-engineer the associations that will attract your ideal client and build content around them.

1, My client wants clarity + structure ← directional orientation ← systems ← tutorials + live builds

2. My client wants someone credible ← authority ← proven results ← testimonials + my story

3. My client wants fulfillment ← alignment with goals ← my story ← my values ← my why

- Example Content Ideas:
    - 90-Day Transformation Series: Document coaching a friend to showcase your process and accountability.
    - Client Check-ins: Post snippets to demonstrate your coaching ability and care.
    - Coaching an Authority Figure: Film a session with a well-known person to build instant authority and curiosity.

### Content as a Reactive Ecosystem

- Principle: Content must be a reactive ecosystem of touchpoints, not a series of isolated posts.
- Balancing Relatability & Authority:
    - Relatability (Mindset/Values): Builds personal connection and resonates with beliefs.
    - Family oriented, grit, its ok to suffer, inputs → outputs, tactical balance, scientific method, engineering mindset, “ship fast and break things”
    - Authority (Value/ICP Pain Points): Establishes expertise and guides the audience toward a solution.
    - Software features/builds/tools, automations, systems, 1:1 client calls, walk throughs, case studies, testimonials,
- Audience Awareness: Use content to move your audience through the 5 stages of awareness (unaware → problem-aware → solution-aware → most aware).
- Diagnosis: If DMs are only supportive, the audience is likely unaware or problem-aware. The next step is to introduce content that calls out specific pain points.

### Practical Application & Q&A

- "My Story" Video: A powerful format for building associations.
    - Function: Brings viewers from problem-aware to solution-aware by showcasing your own transformation as a case study.
    - Action: Update existing "My Story" videos to keep them fresh and relevant.
- Leveraging Existing Associations:
    - Example: "heartbreak gym transformation" association is a powerful asset.
    - Action: Use it as a hook to gain attention before delivering value, rather than trying to eliminate it.
- Identifying Current Associations:
    - Method: Audit your own page from a fresh perspective to see what impressions it creates.
    - Goal: Align your brand with the qualities your ideal client would invest in.
- Effective CTAs:
    - Best Formats: Story sequences and carousels.
    - Rationale: Reel descriptions are rarely read. Story sequences and carousels are more engaging and visible.
