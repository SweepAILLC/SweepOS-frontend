# Hook & Video Concept Ideation SOP
**Category:** Content SOP | **Powers:** Marketing Intel (used as LLM context, personalized by your Intelligence profile)

---

## Universal Framework for Any Coaching Niche

**Purpose:** This is a hook and concept ideation system — not a scripting SOP. It governs what the first 3 seconds say and what idea a video is built around. It does not govern full scripts, editing, or delivery.

**Core problem it solves:** Niche-specific language in the hook caps algorithmic reach before the algorithm can measure broad appeal. This SOP builds hooks wide enough to travel, then filters the viewer down to the ICP inside the video body, so the CTA only converts people who were always going to buy.

**How this SOP is personalized:** Marketing Intel combines this framework with your Intelligence profile — your offer ladder, business description, target audience (ICP), unique selling proposition, and pipeline priorities — so every concept it generates follows this structure *and* is anchored to your specific offer and buyer.

---

## 1. THE CORE PRINCIPLE: THE 3-LAYER FUNNEL

Every piece of content runs through three layers. Confusing the order is the single most common reason content underperforms.

| Layer | Timing | Job | Niche Specificity |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Hook** | 0–3 sec | Stop the scroll for the widest possible relevant audience | ZERO niche terms |
| **Amplifier / Filter** | 3–15 sec | Narrow the universal moment into your specific world | Niche terms introduced here |
| **CTA** | Final 3 sec | Convert only the people who self-identified as ICP | Full niche specificity |

**Why this order, not the reverse:**

- Pattern Interrupt hooks average 72–84% 3-second retention; hooks above 60% retention outperform those below 40% by 5–10x in total reach. Retention is decided before the algorithm knows anything about your niche.
- Loading a niche term ("online coach," "fitness business," "real estate agent") into the first sentence pre-filters your audience to people who already identify with that label — before the hook has done its job of stopping the scroll. You lose the "dream follower" (someone who shares your values/outcomes but doesn't yet identify as your ICP) before they ever see the content.
- The niche filter still has to happen — just later, once retention is already banked. This is what separates "wide reach with no conversion" from "wide reach that filters itself into buyers."

**Operating rule:** If you can swap out one word (fitness coach → real estate agent → nutrition coach) and the hook still works, it passes Layer 1. If it breaks, the niche term entered too early.

## 2. THE 13 HOOK TYPES — GENERALIZED FOR ANY NICHE

Each hook type includes: the psychological driver, the TAM-broadening rule specific to that type, and the same concept applied across three different coaching niches to prove it's structure — not niche-bound.

**Note on the examples:** These are written from the coach's own voice, speaking to their own end client (the person who buys their coaching). Swap the outcome, keep the mechanic, and it still works.

---

### 1. CONTRARIAN HOOK
**Driver:** The brain cannot leave a direct challenge to an existing belief unresolved.
**Broadening rule:** Challenge the *category-level belief* (effort, consistency, talent, more content) before naming the specific mechanism. The belief must be one a broad audience holds, not one specific to your niche's jargon.
- Fitness coaching: "Posting more content isn't why you're not getting clients."
- Real estate coaching: "More listings isn't why your pipeline is dry."
- Relationship coaching: "Being more available isn't why they're pulling away."

---

### 2. CURIOSITY GAP HOOK
**Driver:** Withholding the answer creates information tension the brain must resolve by continuing to watch.
**Broadening rule:** Tease the *result*, not the *industry*. "One thing every top performer does" travels further than "one thing every fitness coach does."
- Business coaching: "There's one thing every $30k/mo coach does in their DMs that most never figure out."
- Nutrition coaching: "There's one thing every client who keeps the weight off does that has nothing to do with food."
- Career coaching: "There's one thing every person who got promoted twice in a year did in their first meeting."

---

### 3. MISTAKE HOOK
**Driver:** Loss aversion + self-identification — fear of being wrong outweighs desire to be right.
**Broadening rule:** Name the *behavior*, not the *title*. "You're doing X wrong" beats "Coaches do X wrong."
- Fitness coaching: "You're onboarding clients wrong. Here's what it's actually costing you."
- Real estate coaching: "You're following up wrong. Here's what it's actually costing you."
- Parenting/education coaching: "You're praising your kid wrong. Here's what it's actually costing you."

---

### 4. RESULT-FIRST HOOK
**Driver:** Promising an outcome without the method creates an information gap that pulls the viewer through.
**Broadening rule:** Lead with the number and timeframe — universally legible — before any niche context.
- Fitness coaching: "My client went from $0 to $12k in 47 days. Here's exactly what we built."
- Dating coaching: "My client went from 0 matches to 3 dates in 2 weeks. Here's exactly what changed."
- Education/course coaching: "My client went from 0 to 200 students in 30 days. Here's exactly what we built."

---

### 5. IDENTITY HOOK
**Driver:** "That's me" is the single most powerful phrase a hook can create — pattern recognition + belonging.
**Broadening rule:** This is the one hook type that is *supposed* to narrow early — but narrow the *situation*, not the *label*. "If you're stuck between X and Y" travels wider than "If you're a [niche title]."
- Fitness coaching: "If you're booked out but not scaling, your backend is the problem. Not your coaching."
- Consulting: "If you're booked out but not scaling, your backend is the problem. Not your expertise."
- Real estate: "If you're booked out but not scaling, your backend is the problem. Not your listings."

---

### 6. FEAR / LOSS HOOK
**Driver:** Loss aversion — people respond roughly 2x faster to potential loss than potential gain.
**Broadening rule:** Anchor the loss in money, time, or falling behind peers — all universal currencies — before naming the mechanism.
- Fitness coaching: "You're losing $4,200/month and you don't even know it."
- Education coaching: "You're losing 40% of your students before module 2 and you don't even know it."
- Real estate coaching: "You're losing 3 deals a month and you don't even know it."

---

### 7. NUMBER / LIST HOOK
**Driver:** Specific numbers create instant structure and credibility; odd, unexpected numbers outperform round ones.
**Broadening rule:** Numbers are already niche-agnostic. Keep the outcome named in the number universal (hours saved, dollars recovered, leads gained).
- Fitness coaching: "4 automations that removed 20 hours of admin from my week."
- Real estate coaching: "4 automations that removed 20 hours of admin from my week." *(identical — the number/outcome IS the hook; niche enters only in the body)*
- Consulting: same structure, same result — the niche only shows up when you name which admin tasks.

---

### 8. SOCIAL PROOF HOOK
**Driver:** Herd behavior + authority bias — proof from a peer outperforms proof from a guru.
**Broadening rule:** Lead with "a [role] just like you," not your own brand name. Peer-level proof travels because the viewer sees themselves in the subject, not in you.
- Fitness coaching: "This coach went from 60% to 88% show-up rate in 7 days. Here's the exact system."
- Course creator coaching: "This creator went from 60% to 88% course completion in 7 days. Here's the exact system."
- Real estate coaching: "This agent went from 60% to 88% appointment show-rate in 7 days. Here's the exact system."

---

### 9. PERSONAL STORY HOOK
**Driver:** Narrative transportation — humans are wired to follow a story to its conclusion. Vulnerability builds trust because it can't be faked at scale.
**Broadening rule:** Open in the conflict, not the backstory or the title. "Two years ago I was manually answering 60 DMs a day" works before the audience knows what you coach.
- Any niche: "Two years ago I was manually answering 60 DMs a day and still losing leads."
- Any niche: "I almost shut the business down in month 3. Here's what stopped me."
- Any niche: "I used to spend Sunday nights terrified I'd forgotten something. Then I built this."

*(This hook type is the most naturally universal — the story format itself does the broadening. Niche context only enters in the amplifier.)*

---

### 10. QUESTION HOOK
**Driver:** The Socratic effect — the brain automatically tries to answer a question, engaging before conscious choice to watch.
**Broadening rule:** Ask the diagnostic question at the outcome level (money, time, growth), not the tactic level.
- Fitness coaching: "How many leads did you lose this week because your DMs were too slow?"
- Legal/consulting: "How many clients did you lose this week because your intake was too slow?"
- Real estate: "How many deals did you lose this week because your follow-up was too slow?"

---

### 11. BOLD CLAIM / OPINION HOOK
**Driver:** Tribal psychology — strong opinions draw both agreement and pushback; both are engagement signals.
**Broadening rule:** Take a stance on the *industry*, not a sub-tactic. Blunt declaratives outperform hedged opinions.
- Fitness coaching: "Most fitness coaches don't have a content problem. They have a positioning problem."
- Real estate: "Most agents don't have a lead problem. They have a positioning problem."
- Course creators: "Most course creators don't have a content problem. They have a positioning problem."

---

### 12. PATTERN INTERRUPT HOOK
**Driver:** Orienting response — the brain automatically redirects attention to anything that breaks expected rhythm. Highest average retention of any hook type (72–84%).
**Broadening rule:** The interrupt is in the *delivery* (silence, mid-sentence start, punchline-first), not the words. This makes it inherently niche-agnostic — swap the noun, keep the structure.
- Any niche: [Silent pause 1 second] "Your [system] is a graveyard because you built a content strategy, not a sales system."
- Any niche: "I'm going to tell you something no one in this space will say out loud."
- Any niche: "Stop. Read this before you post anything else today."

---

### 13. COMPARISON HOOK
**Driver:** Contrast effect — the brain evaluates everything relative to something else. Viewer immediately locates themselves on the spectrum.
**Broadening rule:** Compare universal states (manual vs. automated, $3k vs. $30k, chasing vs. attracting) before naming the niche mechanism.
- Fitness coaching: "The difference between a coach making $3k/month and $30k/month isn't talent. It's this."
- Consulting: "The difference between a consultant making $3k/month and $30k/month isn't expertise. It's this."
- Real estate: "The difference between an agent making $3k/month and $30k/month isn't hustle. It's this."

## 3. THE FILTER LAYER: NICHING DOWN INSIDE THE VIDEO

Once the hook has banked retention, the amplifier (seconds 3–15) does the filtering. This is where the "dream follower" either self-identifies as ICP or opts into a longer nurture path — both outcomes are correct, neither is wasted.

**Filter mechanics, in order:**
1. **Name the specific context.** ("...in your online coaching business" / "...as a real estate agent" / "...running client onboarding.") This is the first place the niche term is allowed to appear.
2. **Name the specific symptom.** Move from the universal loss (money, time) to the mechanism (DM response time, follow-up cadence, onboarding hours).
3. **Position the system, not the tactic.** Show the fix as an architecture (a process, a sequence, a framework) — this is what turns a viewer into a lead, because it signals depth beyond the single tip.

**Awareness-stage mapping (from the 5-stage model):**
- Broad/dream follower viewers are typically unaware or problem-aware — the amplifier should make the invisible problem visible, not pitch a solution yet.
- ICP viewers are typically solution- or product-aware — the amplifier can move faster into system specifics because they already recognize the category.
- One video can serve both: the hook recruits the unaware, the amplifier educates the problem-aware, and the CTA converts the solution-aware. This is intentional, not a compromise.

---

## 4. THE CTA LAYER

The CTA is where niche specificity is total. This is also the qualification mechanism — the comment-gated single keyword ensures only people who watched to the end and cared enough to act enter the DM funnel.

**Rules:**
- One CTA per video. No stacked asks.
- Use a single keyword trigger tied to the specific payload promised in the amplifier, not the hook. ("DM SYSTEMS" for a systems breakdown, "DM SCALE" for a growth teardown.)
- The CTA should feel like the natural next step of the amplifier's specific promise — not a generic pitch bolted onto a broad hook.

---

## 5. CONCEPT IDEATION WORKFLOW

Run this process weekly to generate a content batch. This replaces "coming up with ideas" with a repeatable operational sequence.

**Step 1 — Pick the universal driver.**
Choose one from: money, time, status, fear of falling behind, freedom, identity/belonging. Every hook type maps to one of these.

**Step 2 — Pick the hook type.**
Match hook type to content goal using the performance hierarchy:
- Need reach → Contrarian, Mistake, Identity
- Need watch time → Result-First, Personal Story, Curiosity Gap
- Need DM volume → Identity, Fear/Loss, Social Proof
- Need shares → Bold Claim, Comparison, Number/List

**Step 3 — Write the hook TAM-agnostic.**
Draft it, then run the swap test: substitute your niche term for two unrelated niches. If it still lands, it passes.

**Step 4 — Write the amplifier filter.**
One sentence naming the specific context, one naming the specific symptom, one positioning the system.

**Step 5 — Assign the CTA keyword.**
Tie it directly to the amplifier's specific promise.

**Step 6 — Batch by driver, not by hook type.**
Group a week's content around one universal driver (e.g., all fear/loss week) so the account builds a consistent association without repeating the same hook mechanic twice in a row.

---

## 6. TESTING & DIAGNOSTIC PROTOCOL

**Primary metric: 3-second hook completion rate.** This is measured before niche performance, DM volume, or close rate — it is the leading indicator that determines whether the hook layer is doing its job.

**Protocol:**
- Post the same amplifier + CTA with two different hook types (e.g., Contrarian vs. Personal Story) across a week.
- Compare 3-second retention first, total reach second, DM/comment volume third.
- A hook clearing 60%+ retention with low DM volume is a Layer 1 win, Layer 2 problem — the filter isn't converting. Fix the amplifier, not the hook.
- A hook below 40% retention regardless of amplifier quality is a Layer 1 problem — the niche term likely entered too early, or the driver wasn't universal enough. Rewrite the hook.
