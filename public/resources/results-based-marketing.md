# HOW TO TURN RESULTS INTO MARKETING

let me preface this by saying that this SOP is intended for coaches who know how to get good results. Whether client transformations, cash collected, mental/emotional changes, whatever it is, performance based marketing does not work unless there is true credibility backing it. 

That being said, requesting to utilize those results for marketing is a heavily under utilized and understudied factor of the sales pipeline. Hopefully this short SOP will help guide you in identifying when and how to acquire the assets to begin results based marketing for your coaching biz…

## Step 1: Identifying Wins

After providing the necessary resources and the platform for your clients to succeed, you need a natural mechanism to monitor and discuss their progress. Every coach should be setting up regular check-ins with 1:1 or even group clients in some way or another. Here are some ideas:

- 24/7 text message updates
- Weekly check-in calls
- Weekly check-in forms + custom loom
- Weekly check-in forms + custom email
- Email support
- Leaderboards
- Group check-in calls

These are not only built in opportunities to monitor for wins/successes but also provides more value to your offer. Justifying higher prices for premium high touch levels of support and if you do it well, better testimonials. 

## Step 2: Capitalizing on Moments

Now that you’ve identified your clients wins and successful progress on a regular basis, you need to be ready to capitalize on each opportunity while excitement and trust with the client is at its highest. Here are the best trigger moments to use:

- On a recent win
- Overcoming an obstacle
- Off-boarding
    - frame as a re-cap and feedback session

## Step 3: Testimonial Formats

There are many ways to present a testimonial depending on your offer.  For example, a before and after picture would require an onboarding process marking their beginning state and an onboarding process capturing the effects of the program. Here are a few others below:

- Before/after picture
    - Best for basic posts/carousels on social media and landing page assets
- Video testimonial
    - Best for social media reels and landing page assets
    - client talks about their overall experience, obstacles they overcame, successes, and if they would recommend to someone else
- Client interview (recommended)
    - Best for everything
    - Request a quick interview to highlight their wins and progress in the program. Give you control over how they respond and to what questions
- Basic text testimonial
    - Best for landing page assets
- Data/numbers testimonial
    - Best for landing page assets, social media stories, and social media bio
    - cash collected, weight dropped, etc

All of these are useful and are best leveraged in different areas. However, the client interviews allow you to get the most out of 1 client at once. Try this basic interview framework:

## Step 4: How to ask

Asking for testimonials or interviews should be natural, not forced, and an easily agreeable ask from the client. Try these word tracks:

Before/After pics:
“Really want to be able to track our progress from where we started, are you comfortable doing a before and after photo of your transformation?”

Video/Text testimonial:
”I would love to highlight your story/experience and wins in the program on my page! Would you be open to filming a quick video/answering a few questions about it?”

Data/numbers:
”I would love to highlight your success on my page, is it cool if I share some of the results we’ve generated together?”

Interview:
”I would love to highlight the story of your success on my page. Would you be open to doing a quick 15-20 min interview talking about your experience with me?”

## Conclusion

the reason we ask for testimonials is 2 fold. Firstly, demonstrate authority in our market and niche. If people see we can deliver results, we are instantly more credible. Secondly, we want to intentionally resonate with our ICP. Client interviews and videos do the best because they allow us to market the clients story rather than just their numbers. A lead that watches a testimonial and relates to that persons struggles and aspires for their result will convert WAY easier than one that only reads “Lost 15 lbs in 4 weeks”. 

Stories are what convert. Relatability is how we foster that. 

Think about that as you craft the questions you ask on your forms and interviews as well as who you interview. What struggles is my ICP going through, how can I present this testimonial as a way to give them piece of mind.
