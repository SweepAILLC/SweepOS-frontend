# Objection Handling SOP
**Category:** Sales SOP | **Powers:** Call Library — audits objection handling on every synced call

---

## Purpose

This SOP defines how to **classify, prioritize, and handle** objections on a sales call. Every objection is either **fear** or **logistics**. Fear must be handled **first** — always. Logistics without addressing fear is arguing with a scared person, not closing a sale.

Use this SOP as a **decision tree** during live calls and as the audit standard in Call Library reviews.

---

## The Core Rule

```
ALL OBJECTIONS
      │
      ▼
┌─────────────────┐
│ FEAR or         │
│ LOGISTICS?      │
└────────┬────────┘
         │
    ┌────┴────┐
    ▼         ▼
  FEAR    LOGISTICS
    │         │
    │         └──► Handle ONLY after fear layer is resolved
    │
    └──► HANDLE FIRST — always
```

**Fear objections** = emotional resistance, identity threat, uncertainty, past failure, fear of judgment, fear of wasting money/time, fear of change.

**Logistics objections** = schedule, payment method, contract terms, spouse approval process, technical setup — practical blockers once the person **wants** to move forward.

**Mixed objections** (e.g. "I need to talk to my partner") almost always have a **fear layer underneath** the stated logistics. Handle fear first, then logistics.

## Decision Tree: "I Need to Think About It"

```
"I need to think about it"
         │
         ▼
"What do you need to think about?"
         │
    ┌────┴────────────────────────┐
    ▼                             ▼
Specific concern              Vague / "just need time"
(name it)                          │
    │                              ▼
    │              "You've been waiting and trying [X time] now —
    │               is it fair to your progress to delay success
    │               and kick it down the road further?"
    │                              │
    │                              ▼
    │                        "Why not?"
    │                              │
    │                              ▼
    │              "Your decision-making process needs to change
    │               before you can [achieve their stated goal]."
    │
    └──► Address the specific concern using the relevant tree below
```

**Audit signals — handled well:**
- Salesperson did NOT accept "think about it" at face value
- Asked what specifically needs thinking
- Connected delay to prospect's own timeline and goals from discovery
- Reframed inaction as a decision-making pattern problem

**Audit signals — handled poorly:**
- Accepted "I'll think about it" and scheduled a follow-up without probing
- Argued or pressured without understanding the real concern
- Never referenced goals or time already lost

## Decision Tree: "It's a Lot of Money" / "Big Decision"

```
"It's a lot of money" / "Big decision"
         │
         ▼
   FEAR LAYER FIRST
   (Value / identity reframe)
         │
         ▼
"Expensive compared to what?"
         │
         ▼
"Is it more expensive to stay where you are
 or to pursue change and your goals?"
         │
         ▼
"Would the 2.0 version of yourself that can do
 all the things we talked about choose change
 or stay where they're at?"
         │
         ▼
"What actions do you need to make right now
 to become that person?"
```

**Audit signals — handled well:**
- Reframed cost as investment vs cost of inaction
- Used prospect's own goals and "2.0 self" language from discovery
- Ended with action-oriented question, not a discount

**Audit signals — handled poorly:**
- Jumped straight to payment plans or discounts
- Compared to competitors' prices without value reframe
- Never tied money objection back to their stated pain/goals

## Decision Tree: "Now's Not the Right Time" / Life Circumstances

```
"Now's not the right time" / Life circumstances
         │
         ▼
"That's perfect — that's exactly why we're here."
         │
         ▼
"What in life are you 100% sure about?
 Nothing, right?"
         │
         ▼
"Things will always come up. The way I work with
 clients is how to prioritize their goals even
 when life gets rough."
         │
         ▼
"Is the version of yourself that can accomplish
 all the goals we talked about willing to prioritize
 and work through hard times — or going to give up
 when things get rough?"
         │
         ▼
"What actions do you need to make right now
 to become that person?"
```

**Audit signals — handled well:**
- Reframed timing objection as the reason they need help now
- Used uncertainty of life as argument FOR starting, not delaying
- Connected to identity/goals from discovery

**Audit signals — handled poorly:**
- Agreed timing is bad and rescheduled indefinitely
- Dismissed their circumstances without empathy
- No bridge to goals or identity stakes

## Decision Tree: "What If It Doesn't Work?" / Asks for Testimonials

```
"What if it doesn't work?" / Wants testimonials
         │
         ▼
"Can I ask why you're asking?"
         │
         ▼
"What in life is 100% certain? Nothing, right?"
         │
         ▼
"Are you 100% certain you'll wake up tomorrow?"
         │
         ▼
"If that's the case, is it fair to your progress
 to make assumptions on whether something will work
 unless you try it?"
         │
         ▼
"I want you to make an informed decision based on
 what you think can actually help you — not influenced
 or biased by my best results."
```

**Audit signals — handled well:**
- Explored WHY they're asking (fear of failure, past burns)
- Used uncertainty reframe without being dismissive
- Positioned decision as theirs, based on fit — not hype

**Audit signals — handled poorly:**
- Dumped testimonials without asking why they need them
- Guaranteed results inappropriately
- Got defensive about past client results

## Decision Tree: "I Need to Speak to My Partner"

```
"I need to speak to my partner"
         │
         ▼
"I get that. Do they know we're on the call today?"
         │
         ▼
"Do they support you pursuing your goals?"
         │
         ▼
"How would you envision that conversation going?"
         │
         ▼
"What does your partner do?"
         │
         ▼
"Do they ask for your expertise in that field? Why?"
         │
         ▼
"The same can be said about your situation."
         │
         ▼
"Is it fair to burden them to help you make a choice
 to pursue your own goals and what you know to be
 best for you?"
         │
         ▼
"Why not?"
         │
         ▼
"Because ultimately — whose responsibility is it
 to achieve those goals?"
         │
         ▼
"Then are you ready to take that responsibility and
 ownership over your actions to become that 2.0
 version of yourself?"
```

**Audit signals — handled well:**
- Treated as fear/identity objection, not pure logistics
- Explored partner dynamic without attacking the partner
- Returned ownership to prospect and their stated goals

**Audit signals — handled poorly:**
- Immediately offered "bring them on the next call" without probing
- Pressured prospect to decide against partner's input
- Never connected to prospect's personal responsibility for goals

## Classification Quick Reference

| Objection (surface) | Usually classified as | Handle first |
|---------------------|----------------------|--------------|
| I need to think about it | Fear (decision paralysis) | Fear |
| Too much money | Fear (risk/failure) then logistics | Fear |
| Bad timing / life stuff | Fear (avoidance/identity) | Fear |
| What if it doesn't work | Fear (past failure/trust) | Fear |
| Need to talk to partner | Fear (accountability) + logistics | Fear |
| Send me the contract | Logistics | Logistics (if fear cleared) |
| What day works to start | Logistics | Logistics |

## Objection Handling Score (Audit)

**Fear handled first (1–10):** Did the salesperson address the emotional/fear layer before jumping to logistics, discounts, or scheduling?

**Classification accuracy (1–10):** Did they correctly identify fear vs logistics for each objection raised?

**SOP path adherence (1–10):** For each objection, did they follow the decision tree (key questions asked, reframes attempted)?

**Resolution quality (1–10):** Did objections move the deal forward — or stall it further?

```
Objection Score (0–100) =
  (Fear Handled First × 25)
  + (Classification Accuracy × 25)
  + (SOP Path Adherence × 25)
  + (Resolution Quality × 25)
```

## Audit Checklist (per objection raised)

- [ ] Objection classified as fear, logistics, or mixed
- [ ] If mixed or fear — fear layer addressed before logistics
- [ ] Relevant decision tree path attempted (not generic rebuttal)
- [ ] Prospect's goals/pains from discovery referenced in the reframe
- [ ] Ended with a forward-moving question or clear next step
